Introduction

Consumer awareness refers to a consumer's knowledge of their rights and duties. It helps individuals make informed decisions in the marketplace and protects them from unfair trade practices.

How Consumers Are Exploited

  • Charging more than MRP
  • Selling expired goods
  • False advertising
  • No proper quantity or weight labeling
  • Adulteration in food items

Consumer Protection Act (COPRA) – 1986

This Act was introduced to protect consumers against exploitation. It allows the establishment of consumer courts at three levels:

  • District Forum: For claims up to ₹20 lakhs
  • State Commission: ₹20 lakhs to ₹1 crore
  • National Commission: Above ₹1 crore

Rights of Consumers

  • Right to Safety: Protection against hazardous goods
  • Right to be Informed: About quality, price, ingredients
  • Right to Choose: Access to a variety of products
  • Right to be Heard: Consumer grievances must be considered
  • Right to Seek Redressal: Compensation against unfair practices
  • Right to Consumer Education: Right to be aware of rights

Redressal Process

  1. Keep bill/receipt as proof
  2. Draft a complaint stating the issue
  3. Attach evidence: bill, product photos, warranty
  4. Submit complaint to appropriate consumer forum
  5. Follow up for hearing and judgement
